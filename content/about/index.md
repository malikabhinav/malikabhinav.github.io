## Who I am

I build teams where engineers feel safe, trusted, and empowered to do their best work.
Engineering leader who builds high-ownership teams — where accountability is the culture, not the ask.

My management philosophy is straightforward: set clear expectations, earn trust over time, and make sure every person on my team knows I have their back — unconditionally. I don't believe in micromanagement. Talented engineers already know how to solve hard problems. My job is to remove obstacles, protect the team from organizational noise, and create the space where they can focus.

I lean on my Principal Engineers for technical direction and trust my senior engineers to own their decisions. I ask good questions and stay informed — but I don't pretend to have all the answers. I think that's a feature, not a bug.

I have zero patience for organizational politics. "My team vs your team" thinking is a culture killer. I push hard for collaboration across boundaries and try to be the manager who lifts others rather than one who hoards credit or territory.

Currently at TiVo, I lead engineering delivery across Android, iOS, and Roku for our PayTV streaming platform — managing multiple teams including an offshore contractor relationship on Roku.

## Signature strengths

### People-first leadership
I build trust before I build process. Engineers on my teams know they can bring me any problem without it becoming a performance conversation. That safety net is what unlocks honest communication — which is what unlocks great work.

### Multi-platform delivery breadth
Across TiVo and MobiTV, I have owned delivery across Android, iOS, Roku, Web, and SmartTV platforms simultaneously. I understand what it means to ship on different release cycles, different store review processes, and different device ecosystems — and how to keep multiple teams aligned across all of them.

### Cross-functional bridge-builder
I get pulled into conversations between product, engineering, and business stakeholders when alignment is breaking down. I translate well in both directions and have a low tolerance for "not my problem" energy from any side of the table.

### Technical depth with smart delegation
I came up as a software engineer and still engage actively in architecture reviews, design discussions, and incident post-mortems. I know enough to ask the right questions — and I know when to step back and trust the engineers who live in the codebase every day.
